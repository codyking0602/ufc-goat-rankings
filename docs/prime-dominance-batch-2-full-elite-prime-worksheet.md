# Locked rule

Prime Dominance uses the fighter's **full UFC elite-prime window**.

```text
Prime Dominance = full elite prime
not title-prime only
not championship reign only
```

Use the full elite-prime window for prime record, rounds-won percentage, finish/stoppage rate, loss safety, and sample confidence.

Use title-prime only for the separate 5-point title-defense streak component.

# Fighter notes

## Islam Makhachev

Islam stays as the highest Batch 2 Prime case. The model gives him full lightweight difficulty, an unbeaten elite-prime record, a maxed round-control score, and strong finish credit. The cap is sample length and a shorter title-defense streak than the longest-reign champions.

## Kamaru Usman

Usman was underscored. His full elite-prime round share is 70.8%, and a 5-defense streak is a major marker. Leon and Khamzat context still cap safety, so he does not approach the top-prime tier.

## Cain Velasquez

Cain stays basically where he was. His round control and finish rate are strong, but his UFC defense streak is short and the JDS/Werdum finished losses keep him capped.

## Matt Hughes

Hughes rises because the new formula finally credits the title-defense run and finishing dominance. He still gets a zero in safety because B.J. Penn, GSP, and Alves broke the prime badly.

## Jose Aldo

Aldo gets the biggest correction. UFC-only still gives him a seven-defense run, which the old Prime score was not respecting enough. The Conor/Holloway finish losses keep him from going higher.

## Stipe Miocic

Stipe stays in the same range. His HW title run and finish rate are strong, but the full prime window includes JDS, DC, and Ngannou loss context.

## Daniel Cormier

DC's dominant wins and finish rate help, but full elite prime means the Jon/Stipe rounds count too. That keeps him around 19.

## Charles Oliveira

Charles drops because Prime Dominance is not the same thing as Quality Wins. He has full LW difficulty and big finishing danger, but his title-defense streak is short and the full prime window has too many losses.

## Israel Adesanya

Izzy rises because five title defenses matter. He remains capped because the full elite-prime window is only 60.0% rounds won and includes several title-level losses.
