## Goal

Eliminate preventable audit errors by giving every UFC fighter fact and reviewed judgment call exactly one authoritative owner. Debatable judgment calls remain debatable; stale records, contradictory snapshots, frozen ranks, and duplicate numeric ownership do not.

## Non-negotiable architecture

Permanent data flow:

`canonical UFC fight ledger + reviewed classifications → calculated categories → modifiers → calculated total → calculated sort → calculated rank → calculated OVR → generated snapshot/profile`

Live runtime must never be controlled by:

- `expectedRank`
- `expectedTotalScore`
- `expectedOverallOvr`
- hand-written snapshot stats
- score/stat fields inside fighter packets or display overrides

Expected values may survive only as non-authoritative regression fixtures.

## Why this refactor exists

The July 13 scoring cleanup centralized final score writing but did not centralize fighter facts. Numeric facts still exist across ranking rows, profile rows, scoring records, registries, fighter packets, display overrides, prime records, category ledgers, and profile patches.

The current live scoring file freezes parity and includes expected totals, ranks, and OVR. The scoring engine still consumes those expected values at runtime. That is migration debt and will be removed after canonical facts and calculated categories are ready.

## Canonical ledger schema

`assets/data/canonical-fighter-facts.js` is the future single owner of fight facts and reviewed classifications.

It stores one chronological UFC fight ledger per fighter. Each fight can contain:

- stable fight ID, date, event, opponent, division, and scheduled rounds
- official result and scoring disposition
- method and finish context
- opponent-quality classification and review status
- championship context, eligibility, and reviewed credit inputs
- loss classification, division context, technical exceptions, and review status
- audited round result data when the fight is inside the prime window

The fighter record also stores reviewed model decisions that cannot be derived from official results alone:

- prime start and end fight IDs, or an explicit open prime window
- longevity gap cap and status multiplier
- division-strength key and any date/fight segments
- source coverage and verification date

It explicitly forbids storing derived outputs such as:

- UFC record
- finish rate
- title-fight wins or adjusted title wins
- elite, top-five, or ranked-win totals
- prime record or prime finish totals
- rounds-won percentage
- active elite years
- prime stoppage losses
- through-prime exposure
- category scores, modifiers, total score, rank, or OVR

Those values are calculated from the fight ledger.

Current derivations include:

- official UFC record and model scoring record
- UFC finish wins and finish rate
- official title-fight wins and adjusted title credit
- elite, top-five, and ranked wins plus raw opponent-quality credit
- prime record, prime finish pressure, prime stoppage losses, and rounds-won percentage
- active elite years with the 18-month default gap cap
- through-prime fight exposure and structured loss rows

Round-data rule:

- Every fight inside an `audited` or `live` prime window must have audited round data.
- Pre-prime and post-prime fights may use `rounds.status: "not-audited"` rather than fake zero totals.
- If a prime window later expands, the newly included fights must be audited before the record can pass.

The registry uses deep freezing, rejects duplicate fighter and fight IDs, validates prime boundaries, and validates replacements before changing an existing record.

## Automated enforcement

`scripts/test-canonical-fighter-facts.mjs` is run by the baseline workflow. It proves that:

- a complete audited ledger validates
- aggregate snapshot fields and rank are rejected anywhere in the record
- duplicate fight IDs are rejected
- missing prime endpoints are rejected
- technical exceptions require an explanation
- unaudited rounds are allowed outside the prime but rejected inside it
- fake totals cannot be attached to `not-audited` rounds
- failed replacements leave the prior valid fighter record untouched
- official records and scoring records can differ for technical exceptions
- title, quality, prime, longevity, and exposure values are derived correctly
