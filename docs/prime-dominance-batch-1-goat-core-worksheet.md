## Category purpose

Prime Dominance should answer one question:

```text
During this fighter's best UFC window, how hard were they to beat, how much separation did they create, and how cleanly did they control elite opponents?
```

This is not another title-count category. Championship already handles that.

This is also not another opponent-quality category. OQ already handles who they beat.

Prime Dominance should focus on how dominant the fighter looked while winning.

## Working Prime Dominance formula

Max category score: 30.

| Subcategory | Max | What it measures |
|---|---:|---|
| Prime record / consistency | 7.00 | Winning rate during prime, undefeated windows, lack of weird collapses |
| Round control | 7.00 | Rounds-won percentage, scorecard dominance, five-round control |
| Finish / damage separation | 5.00 | Finishing ability, damage, visible separation without overrating brawlers |
| Elite dominance | 6.00 | How dominant the fighter was against ranked/title-level opponents, not just lower-tier wins |
| Durability / defensive safety | 3.00 | Not being finished, not being badly hurt often, defensive reliability |
| Peak aura / skill separation | 2.00 | Eye-test dominance, tactical control, fear/aura, clean superiority |

Total = 30.

## Guardrails

- Do not double-count titles. Title wins belong mostly in Championship.
- Do not double-count opponent names. Opponent value belongs mostly in OQ.
- Loss penalty still exists separately. Prime Dominance can acknowledge performance-level damage from bad prime losses, but the actual loss deduction lives in Penalty.
- Weird official results need context. Jon Jones' Hamill DQ is not a real competitive loss.
- Upward-division elite losses should not heavily damage same-division Prime Dominance. Volk's Islam losses mostly belong in Penalty context, not a huge Prime Dominance collapse.
- Close wins can still be wins, but repeated close/controversial fights should reduce round-control/separation.
- Finish rate matters, but a control machine should not be punished too hard for not being a finisher if round dominance was overwhelming.

# Main issue this batch exposes

Current Prime Dominance is too harsh on fighters with later elite losses or lower finish rates.

Volk and Max are the clearest examples. Their current prime numbers look like the model is punishing their loss columns instead of isolating their actual prime dominance.

GSP, DJ, Anderson, and Amanda also look underweighted relative to the kind of prime dominance fans actually debate.

Khabib is already basically correct.
