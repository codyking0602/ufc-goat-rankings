# Locked rule

Prime Dominance uses the fighter's **full UFC elite-prime window**.

```text
Prime Dominance = full elite prime
not title-prime only
not championship reign only
```

Use the full elite-prime window for prime record, rounds-won percentage, finish/stoppage rate, loss safety, and sample confidence.

Use title-prime only for the separate 5-point title-defense streak component.

---

# Sample confidence rule

Sample confidence is a Cody-lock field.

The worksheet can show my recommended confidence score, but Cody makes the final call because this component is partly about how much trust the model should place in the prime sample.

# Fighter notes

## Merab Dvalishvili

Merab now edges the batch because his full elite-prime window is strong and current. His title-defense run gives him real dominance credit, and 73.5% rounds won is excellent. The cap is obvious: he is a low finisher, so the stoppage score stays low.

## Chuck Liddell

Chuck rises because the data-first formula likes what he did during the prime: 80.0% rounds won, strong finishing, and a real 4-defense UFC title run. The safety score is still low because the prime ends with Rampage/Jardine/Rashad damage.

## Francis Ngannou

Francis is an elite danger case but not a clean round-control case. The Stipe 1 and Lewis losses drag his round share to 55.0%, and he only has one UFC title defense. His finish score is elite, but the formula does not let finish danger carry the entire category.

## Alex Pereira

Alex rises because his title/finish profile is better than the current Prime score suggests. Still, his Prime Dominance is capped hard by a 51.9% round share and losses to Adesanya, Ankalaev, and Gane.

## Dominick Cruz

Cruz gets a slight bump. He has real title-control moments and solid round winning. He does not rise much because UFC-only removes WEC, his finish rate is low, and Garbrandt/Cejudo cap the full elite-prime window.

## Conor McGregor

Conor is a great short-prime explosion. Cody locked sample confidence at 2.00 because the sample is short but fully proven at the highest level. The formula rewards the finishes and FW/LW difficulty, but no UFC title defenses and two prime finished losses keep him around 18.

## B.J. Penn

B.J. rises because the title-prime and finish profile deserve more respect than 16.27. The full elite-prime record is still messy, and finished losses to Hughes/GSP plus Edgar/Diaz context keep him from going higher.

## Henry Cejudo

Cejudo's two-division Championship Resume is stronger than his Prime Dominance. The full prime has losses to DJ, Benavidez, Sterling, and Merab, so he drops despite the DJ/TJ/Moraes/Cruz wins.

## Randy Couture

Randy's greatness is more Championship Resume and Longevity than clean Prime Dominance. Full elite prime includes too many losses and finished-loss moments, so the formula drops him slightly.

## Petr Yan

Petr is the harshest correction. His current 21.43 Prime score was too high for this formula. The model does not ignore his skill level, but a 7-4 elite-window record, no true title-defense streak, and low finish rate pull him down.
